The Game of Life, originally developed by John Conway, intended to explore cellular automata that functioned on a minimal ruleset without explosive growth or decay capable of supporting heritable mutation. His original 2 dimensional design had three rules


  • A cell with less than two neighbors dies, as if by underpopulation
  • A cell with greater than three neighbors dies, as if by overpopulation
  • A dead cell with exactly three neighbors is brought to life, as if by reproduction

Here is a three dimensional implementation of the game of life. Note how compared to the original two dimensional version the number of automata reaching equilibrium are relatively few, if any. This is because a three dimensional cell has 26 neighbors while, a 2D cell has only 8. These additional dimensions increase the complexity of the system, as well as the instability.
